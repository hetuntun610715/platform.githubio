LAND SUBSIDENCE

INTRODUCTION

The Earth's surface is in a state of continuous movement. Whether it is natural phenomena such as tectonic activities or volcanic eruptions, or human activities like groundwater extraction or mining, the dynamic changes on the surface will have a significant impact on infrastructure and natural ecosystems. In recent years, people's awareness of the potential risks of ground movement has been continuously increasing, so there is an urgent need for comprehensive and reliable ground movement information.

WHY DOES LAND SUBSIDENCE OCCUR?

If local residents over-exploit groundwater, causing the usage volume to exceed the recharge volume, the water level in the aquifer will continuously drop. This will reduce the pore water pressure in the soil and increase the effective stress between soil particles. Eventually, it will lead to soil compaction and consolidation, resulting in ground subsidence.

WHY IS IT THAT PEOPLE HAVE DIFFICULTY NOTICING IT?

The process of land subsidence is extremely slow and continuous. Even in the areas where the land subsidence was the most severe in the past year, it only dropped by 5mm. In the short term, this change would not attract human attention.
